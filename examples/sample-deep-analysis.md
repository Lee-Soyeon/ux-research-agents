### 5.2 Final Themes

**Theme 1: AI as Draft, Not Authority**
- Definition: The user values AI scheduling as a starting point to edit, not a final decision to accept.
- Core insight: Trust in AI features is conditional and earned through transparency and editability.
- Representative utterances: V007 ("like having an assistant who does the first pass"), V004 ("I can't tell why"), V006 ("I was worried it would be locked in")

**Theme 2: Granularity Gap**
- Definition: The app provides broad categorization (Eisenhower quadrants) but lacks the fine-grained control power users expect within those categories.
- Core insight: Familiar frameworks attract power users; missing depth within them drives them away.
- Representative utterances: V008 ("Classic Eisenhower. I like that"), V009 ("Within the quadrant there's no ordering"), V010 ("That feels like a step backward")

**Theme 3: Emotional Feedback Loop**
- Definition: The absence of micro-interactions (completion animations, celebratory feedback) creates an emotional void that undermines task completion motivation.
- Core insight: Productivity apps compete not just on efficiency but on the feeling of accomplishment.
- Representative utterances: V012 ("it's like it never existed"), V013 ("the app doesn't celebrate you finishing something")

**Theme 4: Cognitive Load Reframing**
- Definition: Translating task lists into time estimates transforms overwhelming lists into manageable plans.
- Core insight: The daily summary's value is not informational -- it is emotional (anxiety reduction).
- Representative utterances: V014 ("When I just see a list of 6 things I feel overwhelmed but with the time estimate it's like, okay, I can handle that"), V015 ("I'd check this every morning for sure")

**Theme 5: Selective Evangelism**
- Definition: The user actively plans to share impressive features while hiding incomplete ones.
- Core insight: Users curate their recommendations. A single strong feature can drive word-of-mouth even if other features are weak.
- Representative utterances: V020 ("My freelancer friends for sure"), V021 ("I'd literally screen-record the rearranging animation"), V022 ("I wouldn't show them the priority matrix yet")

### 5.3 Theme Map

```
Central Theme: AI as Draft, Not Authority
    ├── enables → Cognitive Load Reframing (daily summary as AI-curated overview)
    ├── undermined by → Granularity Gap (AI categorizes but cannot prioritize within)
    └── drives → Selective Evangelism (users share what the AI does well)

Supporting Theme: Emotional Feedback Loop
    └── independent of AI but critical to retention
```
